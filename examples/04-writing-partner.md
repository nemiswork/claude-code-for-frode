# Example 4: Writing Partner

> Time: ~10 minutes
> Goal: Use Claude Code for academic writing, administrative communications, and reports

## Academic Writing

### Abstract Writing

```
I need an abstract for a paper with these key findings:
- We identified 47 differentially expressed proteins in CSF from early-stage AD patients
- Top hit: SMOC1 (3.2-fold increase, p < 0.001)
- Validation cohort confirmed 12/47 proteins
- Machine learning classifier achieved 91% AUC for early AD detection
- Study used TMT-16plex on Orbitrap Eclipse, n=120

Write a 250-word structured abstract (Background, Methods, Results, Conclusions)
for Journal of Proteome Research.
```

### Improve Existing Text

Save any text you've written to a file, then:

```
Read my-draft.txt. Improve the writing:
- Fix grammar and awkward phrasing
- Strengthen the scientific argumentation
- Keep it concise — remove filler
- Preserve my voice and style
- Show me a diff of what you changed and why
```

## Administrative Writing

### Faculty Communication

```
Draft an email to all faculty members at the Department of Biomedicine about:
- New policy on lab safety training (mandatory completion by June 1)
- Upcoming department retreat (September 12-13, Solstrand Hotel)
- Budget submissions due April 15
- Tone: professional but warm, I'm the Head of Department
- Keep it under 300 words
```

### Committee Reports

```
I need to write a yearly report for the Faculty Board covering our department's activities:
- 12 new PhD candidates started
- 3 PhD defenses completed
- 2 new externally funded projects (total 25M NOK)
- PROBE platform served 45 research groups
- New equipment: Orbitrap Astral acquired
- Student evaluations: 4.2/5.0 average

Structure it formally with sections. Make it factual and concise.
```

### Meeting Preparation

```
I have a budget meeting with the Faculty Dean tomorrow. Help me prepare:
- Our operating budget is 82M NOK
- We're requesting 5M increase for new technician positions
- Main argument: PROBE demand has tripled, current staffing is unsustainable
- Anticipate counterarguments and prepare responses
- Create a one-page talking points document
```

## Recommendation Letters

```
Write a recommendation letter for a PhD candidate applying for a postdoc at ETH Zurich:
- Name: [Candidate Name]
- Worked with me for 4 years on CSF proteomics
- 3 first-author papers in high-impact journals
- Developed novel sample preparation workflow
- Strengths: methodological rigor, independent thinking, great collaborator
- Tone: strong, specific, enthusiastic
- Address to: Professor [Name], Institute of Molecular Systems Biology
```

## Quick Text Tasks

### Peer Review

```
I'm reviewing a manuscript on plasma proteomics for cancer diagnostics.
Here are my rough notes:
- Methods solid but missing important controls
- Sample size too small (n=20)
- Statistics questionable — no multiple testing correction
- Good idea, poor execution
- Figure 3 is misleading

Turn these notes into a constructive, professional peer review with
major and minor points.
```

### Presentation Text

```
Create speaker notes for a 15-minute presentation on:
"The Future of Clinical Proteomics: From Discovery to Diagnostics"
- Audience: hospital clinicians (not proteomics experts)
- Key message: proteomics is ready for clinical translation
- Include 3-4 compelling examples
- End with what we're doing at UiB/PROBE
```

## What You Just Learned

- Claude adapts its writing style to the context (academic, administrative, formal)
- It can improve existing text while preserving your voice
- It handles Norwegian academic culture and conventions
- Draft → refine → finalize is the natural workflow
